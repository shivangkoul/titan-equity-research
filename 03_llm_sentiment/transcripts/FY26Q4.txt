Titan Company Limited — Q4 & FY'26 Earnings Conference Call — May 08, 2026
Source: https://www.titancompany.in/sites/default/files/2026-05/SEIntimationtranscriptq4.pdf
Speakers: Ajoy Chawla (MD), Ashok Sonthalia (CFO), Arun Narayan (CEO Jewellery), Kuruvilla Markose (CEO Watches), Raghavan N S (CEO EyeCare), Saumen Bhaumik (MD CaratLane)

Ajoy Chawla: Thank you. Warm welcome to the Titan Q4 FY26 Earnings Call. Thank you for being here this Friday afternoon. We have ended the year with a superlative Quarter 4 top-line growth, perhaps our best-ever in the recent past. All our businesses have grown very well, and all our brands have enhanced their visibility and equity in the quarter and the full year that went by. Over to you for all the questions so we can get on with the Q&A.

Devanshu Bansal (Emkay Global): Congratulations on a good set of numbers. Firstly, I wanted to check on gold sourcing. The industry sources a considerable part of gold from Middle East and there was some news flow that there has been delay in the renewal of import licenses by DGFT. I am checking if you could provide some color on the current inventory levels as well as any increasing costs related to sourcing that you foresee in the near term.

Ashok Sonthalia: Hi, Devanshu. While, yes, some of those slowness we are also picking up from the customs point of view, but we are pretty much quite covered for Quarter 1. And also our gold exchange program is very, very successfully being run from the last Quarter 3 onwards. We always used to run this, but now we have another level of another short notice we can, if required, further ratchet it. And some of the other plan Bs are also ready. So, we are at least not concerned in the short term as far as gold supply is concerned. For Titan, Tanishq and Caratlane perspective.

Devanshu Bansal: Got it. Just a small follow up. From an availability perspective I understood, but do you foresee any increase in cost? Maybe our gold metal loan cost was around 3%-odd. Do you foresee an increase there?

Ashok Sonthalia: No, not yet. Now, if you would recall, gold loan tenure has been increased from 180 to 270 days. And we are working on that, and we are successful in starting that also. So, we don't see any increase in cost in gold loans, at least in the short term.

Devanshu Bansal: Sir, Damas is a recent acquisition. International loss has been around ₹80 crores in Q4. If you could guide us on the top line scale growth as well as near-term loss run rate for Damas, it would be helpful for forecasting FY27.

Ashok Sonthalia: One of the positive news for international business, if you look at the full year, at operating level, they became profitable. This is the first year after we started and scaled it up. So, on a full year basis, it is profitable. Q4 certainly had challenges. March month was quite disturbed in the GCC, where not only Damas is present, but our Tanishq also is present. So, all those things have come in Q4 in the form of loss of ₹82 crores. GCC continues to be an evolving and unpredictable situation where the Damas vast retail network is there and Tanishq is also expanding there through conversion of Damas retail network. A lot of restructuring is going on. But over the next two, three quarters beyond, we are very, very positive about the integration, the operational improvement. Damas for the next four quarters — we started consolidating from January. We will have increased disclosure coming out as we get better. Whatever plan they had, they have actually done better than that plan.

Devanshu Bansal: The unallocated losses, which used to be around ₹20-₹30 crores, this time around is around ₹140 crores in Q4. What has led to this higher loss?

Ashok Sonthalia: There was quite a good performance for the full year. And Titan, in the past also, we have a tradition of sharing that superlative performance — we call it special reward, which is announced once we know the result. This year also the good performance has been celebrated by sharing that with all the employees and all the people connected with Titan. So, it is about ₹100-₹120 crores roughly. That is the variation you are seeing.

Aditya Soman (CLSA): You said a lot of that ₹82 crores loss was not in Damas. So that could be for Titan's Middle East businesses?

Ashok Sonthalia: Yes, you are right. It is the international subsidiary. Although we had a transfer pricing arrangement from the start of international business, we formalized that from '25-26, where we are now treating all our subsidiaries as low-risk distributor and Titan as a parent company is ensuring a certain amount of basic profit. That transfer pricing adjustment in the standalone books is about ₹80 crores plus minus, but on consolidated basis that nullifies because it is between subsidiaries and the parent.

Kuruvilla Markose: Analog watches overall growth has been around 16% for the year. If you look at both smart and analog put together, because smart reduced in numbers, overall growth has been about 14% for the Watches division. Analog within the watch is around 85%, now closer to 90%.

Mihir Shah (Nomura): Congrats on a good set of numbers. First question is on buyer growth. I wanted to get a sense of what led to the return of buyer growth to 8% versus flat that we have seen for the past nine months.

Arun Narayan: This is Arun Narayan. Very clearly, we have seen resurgence in buyer growth in Quarter 4 — 8% versus a flattish for the period prior. Two clear types of buyers: those who were waiting on the sidelines, and we have seen gold rates go up from festive onwards, so many customers who were waiting came in to buy in Quarter 4. And we have also seen an advancement in wedding purchases because people were anxious that gold could go up even beyond. We have also seen a resurgence in buyer growth on the studded front, aided by the festival of diamonds, which has been a very successful campaign. The continued exchange campaign, which we began just before festive, really has worked extremely well. So on both sides, we have seen good buyer growth.

Mihir Shah: How do you see the sustainability of this? Do you think the exchange program will help ensure some level of buyer growth to continue?

Arun Narayan: We think so. We believe so. That's why we are sustaining the investment behind exchange. It's very relevant for wedding buyers and for others looking at updating their collection. The sentiment of the campaign has resonated very well. So, we think it is a good thing to run and it will give us rewards going forward.

Mihir Shah: On profitability, you have been within the 11-11.5% band in the TMZ side of the business in domestic. Any confidence on sustaining these levels?

Ashok Sonthalia: Gold price is playing a significant role on the product mix aspect, and consequently it is very difficult to tell sustainability. If gold remains at the current rate, it is sustainable. But if gold continues to go up, we may have to keep making effort — 18-carat, 14-carat jewellery, mix re-engineering, lightweight jewellery — a lot of things the team has been doing. So to some extent you can offset that, but beyond a point there will be impact on margin. Some 10-20 basis points we have been losing, and the other thing we are able to offset sometimes through control of overhead costs. I would not give you a good assurance that this is sustainable. We will talk more at our Investor Day in June first week, in the context of where growth is number one priority.

Kunal Vora (BNP Paribas): Gold price is now stabilizing at a fairly high level. How is the consumer managing with the high gold price?

Arun Narayan: All of the above. People have started accepting gold at this level. The small cool-off in the last month or two has also helped bring people back, because there seems to be an acceptance that in the medium to long term it's again going to go back to its upward trajectory. We need to make jewellery both exciting and accessible — exchange, 18-carat gold, 14-carat studded jewellery, the Gramage Purchase Plan, and lightweight. There is interest in the category, which is what we saw in Quarter 4 by way of buyer growth in both diamond studded and gold jewellery. We are also expanding the pie with the natural gemstone 'Hues' collection launched this Akshaya Tritiya.

Kunal Vora: FY26 was a strong year and the base will keep getting higher. Can you share early thoughts on FY27 jewellery sales growth, and how confident are you of maintaining at least 20% growth?

Arun Narayan: In the past, we have said between 15% to 20%. That holds good and we hope to do that and maybe a bit better. Preponement can keep happening because wedding dates are sustained. It all depends on the trajectory of gold and its impact on consumer sentiment. Too early to call, but we are hopeful to keep to the same guidance.

Ajoy Chawla: The fundamentals of the jewellery category and the industry formalizing — organized players with stronger balance sheets growing more rapidly, brands providing trust and differentiation. That formalization continues to be a very strong underlying factor for growth, along with the India growth story. Therefore the 15% to 20% is something that we ought to do. If we don't do that much, we won't be doing justice to all these tailwinds. Caveats will always exist in terms of volatility in gold price and macro sentiment, but those need to be managed.

Ajoy Chawla (closing): Thank you, everyone, once again, for a very engaging and interesting conversation. Thanks for all your questions. They make us think and look forward to seeing you soon in the next few weeks. Till then, take care.
